Book Covers

RadioLab Trilogy

Challenging actions through uncomfortable perspectives

WNYC’s RadioLab was in need of book covers that reflect three different podcasts: Morality, Stress, and Help.

Skills
  • Concept Sketches
  • Photography
  • Typography
  • Layout
  • Print
the challenge

Translating ideas into visual language

Deliver the essence of three different RadioLab podcasts—titled Morality, Stress, and Help—as visually and conceptually cohesive book cover trilogy.

the outcome

Refining experiences through emotionally-charged scenes

Provoking, acting-upon, and processing one’s emotions during jarring events is an ongoing theme in these podcasts. To manifest this theme, I created unnerving imagery, typography, and layout- design to evoke the viewer to respond and reflect on their understanding of the respective book title.

Sketches

These are the final ideas for my sketches. I chose two routes for each title:

In the first set of iterations, I chose to interpret the topics metaphorically—represented by the tree, chain and scissors, and elevator buttons.

The second set of iterations is depicted from the perspective of the viewer, which places them in a situation that asks them to think about the respective topic—represented by the boiling pot, cake and hole.

First Draft

I presented three styalistic iterations of my bookcovers. I sketched, sculpted, and photographed these cakes. In the end, the photographic-style was chosen due to its eary undertones the feel of the podcast.

Final Designs

Morality

According to the podcast, morality is based on one’s definition of fairness, which provokes a battle between empathy and logic. The interviewees in this podcast spoke of events in which they either partook or witnessed immoral acts that stuck in their memory and questioned their sense of morality. 

I sought to visually interpret their collective experiences by disrupting one's sense of fairness. Portraying the unjust distribution of something that is meant to be shared amongst others, forces the viewer to witness the uncomfortable act. In doing so, the viewer is faced with the slice of cake on the back-cover.

Stress

The podcast discusses the process and outcomes of stressful circumstances and the variety of responses that can lead to success or breakdown. Ultimately, the outcome is contingent on how one deals with ones’ emotions.

Depicting the volatile-nature of stress through a first-person perspective immediately places the viewer in a situation that allows for an intimate portrayal of thoughts and emotions. As such, I effectively communicate how stress can make one feel. Even in one’s mental disarray, one, in fact, has the power to find mental clarity and turn the heat down

Help

The podcast discusses different approaches people used to push through mental blocks and self-sabotaging behavior. Some interviewees describe situations in which fear—induced by self-threat—is the driving factor to force them out of their struggles.

Putting the viewer in a dark, confined space amplifies the struggle that one undertakes when trying to break-away from their problem. The figure looking down upon the viewer creates an unsettling, ambiguous atmosphere to represent the self-induced threat that haunted the interviewees to reach their goal by pulling themselves out.
