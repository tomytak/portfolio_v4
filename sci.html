Science Walk

The Science Walk and the Waimea Solar System Walk are a Youtube series in a set created by the Canada-France-Hawai‘i Telescope. These series aims to educate viewers on different aspects of our world and the universe by hosting a collection of videos from different organizations that talk about their respective topic. The Science Walk, in particular, focuses on Earth sciences and conservation.

In order to adjust to COVID-19 regulations, the walk consists of a physical outdoor path in Waimea, Hawai‘i hosted by the Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope. On the path, attendees can view the educational videos–via QR code.

I created all of the opening and closing scenes along with the motion graphics in the videos titled: Volcanos, Our Atmosphere, Our Sun, Astrobiology, and JWST. The motion graphics in the videos titled: Humpback Whales, Native Birds, and The ‘Alala Project were created by their respective organizations.

Finalized Design
Original logo

In order to maintain the integrity of the set, creating a clear visual connection between both series was my top priority. Therefore, keeping the Waimea Solar System Walk’s logo theme, “opening new doorways" was essential in maintaining the continuity between both designs. This was done by implementing the same doorframe and walkway.

In order to portray the wide-range of Earth science topics, illustrating the land, sea, and space aided in individualizing this logo.

Animations

The goal, especially within the opening graphics, is to unite the various topics cohesively through the storyline. To accomplish this, I created mini-animations that represent each respective topic. This resulted in an adventure through the ocean, land, and into space.
